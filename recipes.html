Oyakodon

Comfort in a bowl! Oyakodon, a classic Japanese dish, brings together tender chicken, soft onions, and fluffy eggs simmered in a savory-sweet dashi sauce, all served over steamy rice. Translating to "parent-and-child bowl" (a nod to its chicken-and-egg combo), this dish is hearty, quick to make, and oh-so-satisfying. Perfect for a cozy night in or a speedy yet delicious meal! 🍚🥚🐓

Ingredients

  • ½ onion (4 oz, 113 g; peeled)
  • 10 oz boneless, skinless chicken thighs (1-2 thighs; use plant-based meat substitute for vegan/vegetarian)
  • 1 Tbsp sake (for marinating the chicken; substitute with Chinese rice wine or dry sherry, or omit)
  • 3-4 large eggs (50 g each w/o shell; for vegan/vegetarian, use egg substitute like JUST Egg)
  • ½ cup dashi (Japanese soup stock; use standard Awase Dashi, dashi packet/powder, or Vegan Dashi)
  • 2 Tbsp soy sauce
  • 2 Tbsp mirin
  • 2 tsp sugar
  • 2 servings cooked Japanese short-grain rice (typically 1⅔ cups (250 g) per donburi serving)
  • 4 sprigs mitsuba (Japanese parsley) or green onion/scallion
  • shichimi togarashi (Japanese seven spice; optional)
  • Japanese sansho pepper (optional)
